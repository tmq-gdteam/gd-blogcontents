# LinkedIn Post

Most of my career has started with the same question:

Why doesn't this exist yet?

Not because I wanted to be first. Because the thing seemed like it should be possible.

That mindset took me from writing assembly code as a kid, to building networking systems, wireless connectivity, ISP infrastructure, business text messaging, voice AI, and now agentic AI.

The lesson has stayed surprisingly consistent: the market often rejects an idea before it has language for it.

People laughed at two-way business texting. Now it feels obvious.

People said certain streaming, networking, and voice AI experiences were not practical. Some of them just needed better systems, more patience, and a team willing to ask "how?" longer than other people were willing to.

The same principle matters even more with AI.

I do not believe the best future is AI that helps people create more shallow output faster. The better future is AI that takes on the repetitive load machines are good at, so people can spend more time on creativity, problem-solving, and critical thinking.

That is where useful technology lives: not in replacing human value, but in clearing space for more of it.
