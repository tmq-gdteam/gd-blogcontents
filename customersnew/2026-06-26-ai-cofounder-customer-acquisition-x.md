# X / Twitter Thread — Business Physics Just Broke

**1/**
For 100 years, business growth was just physics:

Want to double revenue? Double the headcount.

That law is breaking. Today, doubling headcount might be the fastest way to go broke 🧵

**2/**
Two founders, two traps:

Non-technical → hand 20–50% equity to a technical co-founder before you have a single customer.

65% of high-potential startups die from co-founder conflict. 43% end up buying the partner out.

**3/**
Solopreneur → the opposite problem.

48% work 50+ hrs/week. ~25% never take a day off.

Your stamina becomes the literal ceiling on revenue. Sleep 8 hrs, the business stops for 8 hrs.

**4/**
Both lose revenue to the same thing: buyers with zero patience.

• 54-sec attention span
• 5-min response = ~21x more likely to convert than 30 min
• 78% buy from the FIRST responder
• <1 min = up to 391% conversion lift

**5/**
The fix isn't a faster chatbot.

A chatbot = receptionist with amnesia.
An agentic, stateful agent = executive assistant with photographic memory + the keys.

Lead at 2am? It qualifies, checks your calendar, drafts a proposal, books the meeting. While you sleep.

**6/**
The economics:

~$0.50 per automated conversation vs ~$6 for a human.

US new business apps surged to 5.1–5.5M/yr — while traditional W-2 hiring stalled. Founders are skipping the hiring phase entirely.

**7/**
But speed = a leaky bucket.

Retention is ~5x cheaper than acquisition (60–70% vs 5–20% close odds).

You can't automate it on broken data — 76% of companies admit half their CRM is wrong.

**8/**
One rule that never bends: human-in-the-loop on anything irreversible.

Fund transfers. Refunds. CRM overwrites. Mass sends.

A hallucinated refund policy can bankrupt you in seconds.

**9/**
When every competitor runs the same flawless 24/7 operation, "efficient" is just the boring baseline.

The AI can build the house faster than ever.

Making it a home is still entirely on you.
