# LinkedIn Post — Business Physics Just Broke

For a century, growth was just physics: want to double revenue? Double the headcount. More reps, more managers, more payroll.

That law is breaking. Today, doubling headcount to double revenue might be the fastest way to go broke.

Two kinds of founders are trapped by the old rules:

→ The non-technical founder. To get an MVP built, the market demands handing 20–50% of the company to a technical co-founder before a single customer exists. And 65% of high-potential startups fail from co-founder conflict — not funding, not product-market fit. 43% eventually buy their technical partner out.

→ The solopreneur. 48% of small business owners work 50+ hours a week and ~25% never take a day off. Their stamina is the literal ceiling on revenue.

One bleeds equity. One bleeds time. Both lose revenue to the same thing — buyers with zero patience:

• 54-second attention span on a website
• A 5-minute response is ~21x more likely to convert than 30 minutes
• 78% buy from the FIRST vendor to respond
• Sub-1-minute response → up to 391% conversion lift

The fix isn't a faster chatbot. It's agentic, stateful AI — an executive assistant with a photographic memory and the keys to the filing cabinet. A lead lands at 2 a.m.; it qualifies them, checks your calendar, drafts a proposal, and books the meeting while you sleep. ~$0.50 per automated conversation vs ~$6 for the human equivalent.

But speed creates a leaky-bucket problem. Retention is ~5x cheaper than acquisition (60–70% odds of selling to an existing customer vs 5–20% for net-new) — and you can't automate it on broken data (76% of companies admit half their CRM is inaccurate).

One rule that doesn't bend: keep a human in the loop on anything irreversible — fund transfers, refunds, CRM overwrites, mass sends. Automate the back office to buy back your calendar, then spend that time on the empathy and judgment AI can't fake.

Because when every competitor runs the same flawless 24/7 operation, "efficient" becomes the boring baseline. The AI can build the house faster than ever. Making it a home is still on you.
