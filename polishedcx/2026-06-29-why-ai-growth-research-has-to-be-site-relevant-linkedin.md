# LinkedIn Post

Most AI growth advice sounds impressive before it becomes useful.

That is the problem.

A founder can paste in a website and get strategy in seconds. But unless the research separates evidence from inference, identifies the real buyer pressure, and names what should not be automated, it is just confident theater.

Site-relevant AI research has to do something stricter.

It should treat the website as evidence.
It should distinguish verified claims from hypotheses.
It should separate the aspiring founder from the overloaded operator.
It should connect product features to operational pressure.
It should explain where automation helps and where human judgment still matters.

That matters especially in categories like real-time voice AI, where the product is not simply "better audio." The real business case is reducing communication friction in high-stakes calls, preserving trust, and helping companies scale global teams without weakening customer experience.

The best AI research will not be the loudest or fastest output.

It will be the work that helps founders see what is true, what is assumed, and what deserves action next.
