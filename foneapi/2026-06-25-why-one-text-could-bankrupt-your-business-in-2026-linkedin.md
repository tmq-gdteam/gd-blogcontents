# LinkedIn Post

A single automated text message can now cost a business up to $10,000.

That sentence would have sounded absurd a few years ago. Today it's just the price of ignoring how much the rules have changed underneath us.

Building communication software has never been easier. A founder can ship a messaging MVP in a weekend. A local business owner can automate missed-call follow-ups without hiring an engineer. The keys to powerful infrastructure are sitting right there.

But the road got more dangerous while the cars got faster.

Sending business SMS from a standard number now means registering your brand and campaigns through A2P 10DLC before a single message goes through. Outbound calls without proper STIR/SHAKEN attestation get stamped "Spam Risk" before anyone says hello. OTP forms left unprotected become open taps for SMS pumping fraud. And consent isn't solved just because one system logged an opt-out. If marketing, sales, and support don't sync, you're still exposed.

Here's the part most people miss: a messaging workflow is no longer a feature. It's a regulated operational surface. The copy, the timing, the recipient list, the opt-in path, all of it carries risk.

The answer isn't to avoid automation. It's to demand more from the platforms you build on. Raw APIs and cheap pricing aren't enough anymore. Compliance, fraud protection, and consent control have to be part of the foundation, not an afterthought.
